Recommendations of books

I have read over quarantine

Six of Crows by Leigh Bardugo, is a YA Fantasy novel in a duology. I read it over quarantine and may I say, this is one of the most enthralling and fast-paced books I have ever read. The fictional world is so fresh compared to other YA Fantasy worlds, and I really enjoy the aspect of Magic. The overall idea of the book is a magical money heist.
Crooked Kingdom by Leigh Bardugo, is a YA Fantasy sequel to Six of Crows. I was really sad when this book ended, not only because of the ending but also because it ended. I wish there was a third book because the characters were so lovable and it's so sad they only got two books for character development.
Norwegian Wood by Haruki Murakami, is a Japanese coming-of-age novel. This book is SO poetic and very emotionally mature. This is the best written book I have ever read. The writing style was really the focal point of this novel, and it isn't so prevalent in american novels. As cheesy as it is, I feel like this book changed me and how I view the world. Definitely a very intense book emotionally, so I would recommend it for 15/16+
The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes by Suzanne Collins, is a prequel to the Hunger Games trilogy. This book focuses on the antagonist of the main series, President Snow, in his adolescent years starting at the 5th annual Hunger Games. It's very interesting how it is written with him as the protagonist, in an attempt for the reader to sympathize with the character, even though he is known to be the antagonist in the main series.
